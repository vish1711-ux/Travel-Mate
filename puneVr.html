Pune

Shaniwar Wada, initially intended as a seven-story stone building, was completed in 1732 under Peshwa Baji Rao I, who laid its foundation on 10 January 1730. Due to objections from the national capital, the structure was made of brick above the base floor. By 1758, it housed around a thousand people. The fort is known for its historical events and legends. In 1773, Peshwa Narayanrao was murdered, and a popular legend claims his ghost calls for help on full moon nights. In 1818, Peshwa Bajirao II abdicated and went into exile. A great fire in 1828 destroyed much of the palace, leaving only the ramparts and foundations. Shaniwar Wada was constructed with materials imported from various regions: teak from Junnar, stone from Chinchwad, and lime from Jejuri. The fort's perimeter includes five gateways and nine bastion towers, and it is situated near the Mula-Mutha River in Kasba Peth. The complex had an impressive lotus-shaped fountain: the Hazari Karanje (Fountain of a thousand jets). It was constructed for the pleasure of the infant Peshwa Sawai Madhavrao. It was designed as a sixteen petal lotus; each petal had sixteen jets with an eighty-foot arch. It was the most complicated and intricate fountain of its time.[3] Shrimant Anandrao Rudrajirao Dhulap-More (an admiral of Maratha navy in 18th century) who visited the Shaniwarwada in 1791 described it as "very magnificent. A hundred dancers can dance here at a time. In one corner is a marble Ganapati statue and the palace is flanked by a fountain and a flower garden.".
